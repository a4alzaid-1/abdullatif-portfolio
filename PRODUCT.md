# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and admissions reviewers for competitive technical training programs
and scholarships (e.g. the Huawei × KFAS AI training program in China) — people
scanning quickly for evidence of real, shipped technical work, not a list of
claimed skills. Secondary audience: potential freelance clients evaluating
Abdullatif's web-design work.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio site for Abdullatif Alzaid, a Software Engineering student
in Kuwait, whose core practice is directing AI coding tools to design, build,
and ship real production websites for real small-business clients — end to
end, including outreach, pricing, and delivery. Success looks like a reviewer
clicking through to a live, working client site within seconds and coming away
convinced this is real shipped work, not a class project or a template.

## Brand Personality

Innovative, direct, curious. Confident and opinionated rather than safe or
generic — the site should read as someone who makes a deliberate craft choice
and commits to it, not someone hedging toward whatever looks inoffensive.

## Anti-references

Explicitly must not look like a generic AI-generated site: no cream/sand body
background, no gradient text, no tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow above every
section, no numbered section markers as default scaffolding (01/02/03...)
used as filler, no identical icon+heading+text card grids, no glassmorphism,
no hero-metric template. Also avoid the generic bootcamp-portfolio template
look (soft pastel SaaS-developer-portfolio aesthetic) that every other
graduating student's site tends toward.

## Design Principles

- Commit to one deliberate aesthetic system fully rather than blending safe defaults.
- Every project shown must link to something real and live — no placeholder screenshots standing in for working software.
- Say plainly what was AI-directed vs. personally owned (design, testing, delivery, client management) — precision over vague "AI-powered" branding.
- Bold and confident, but never at the cost of a reviewer being able to find the resume, the projects, and a way to contact him within seconds.
- Content honesty: never overstate technical skills (e.g. hand-coding proficiency) that haven't actually been demonstrated.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA. No stated specific user accessibility needs; support
`prefers-reduced-motion` and maintain body-text contrast ratios per general
best practice.
